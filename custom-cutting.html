Custom Meat Cutting

Custom cuts, coordinated through our Amery facility

Half hogs, quarter cows, custom steaks, and specialty cuts. We handle the order here in Spooner — the cutting happens at our sister store Amery Meat Market — and your order is ready for pickup at 118 Ash Street.

Custom cuts are coordinated through our sister store Amery Meat Market — same Lutz family, same Wisconsin standards. You place the order here in Spooner, they cut it fresh to your specs at the Amery facility, and your order is ready for pickup at 118 Ash Street. Drop in or call ahead to get started — most cuts ready within a few days.

What we cut to order

  • Quarter / half cow (bring your hanging beef or order through us)
  • Half hog (smoking, sausage, fresh cuts)
  • Custom steak thicknesses — 1", 1.25", 1.5", 2"
  • Custom roasts (tied, netted, or specialty trims)
  • Specialty cuts: tomahawk, Denver, teres major, hanger

How it works

  1. Stop in or message us on Facebook with your request
  2. We confirm specs and coordinate the cut with our sister store Amery Meat Market
  3. They cut, vacuum-seal, and label to your specifications at the Amery facility
  4. Pick up at 118 Ash Street, Spooner, WI — typically ready within a few days

Pricing

Custom cuts are priced per pound at our standard rates — no premium for the spec work. Half hog and quarter cow are quoted per animal based on hanging weight. Looking to stock the freezer? Our Freezer Beef Package starts at $40, with bulk pork packages and custom meat bundles also available. Ask us for a current quote.

Frequently Asked Questions

How much notice do you need for a custom order?

For standard cuts, give us a day or two. For half hog or quarter cow orders, a few days helps us coordinate with our sister store Amery Meat Market. Call or message us on Facebook to confirm timing.

How is my order packaged?

Every cut is vacuum-sealed and labeled at our sister store Amery Meat Market. Pickup is at 118 Ash Street in Spooner — bring a cooler for the drive home if you're not heading straight to your freezer.
