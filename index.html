Family-Owned & Hands-On

A family-owned butcher and cellar, rooted in Spooner.

Crossroads of the North Meats and Cellar is the work of Jen and Dennis Lutz — a family-owned shop built for a town they love. Spooner sits at the crossroads of Wisconsin's railroad history, lake country, and working farms, and that heritage runs through everything we do: hand-cut meat, house-made brats, and a curated cellar picked with care.

We're putting the finishing touches on 118 Ash Street, right in the heart of downtown. Our plan is simple — bring back the kind of local butcher and corner store where you know who's behind the counter, where the brats are made in-house, and where the cheese and wine were chosen by someone who actually tried them.

Jen and Dennis are committed to being hands-on partners with their team and their community. They can't wait to open the doors and welcome you in.

Now Hiring

Help us open the doors.

We're putting the team together for opening day. If working at a local, family-owned butcher and cellar sounds right to you, we'd love to hear from you.

Full-Time Reports to: Jen & Dennis

Store Manager

Lead daily operations, our team, product quality, and the customer experience. Be the first manager of a brand-new Spooner business.

  • What you'll do: Open and close the store, supervise staff, train new hires, oversee meat case presentation and product rotation, manage inventory and vendor relationships, operate the POS, and keep the store clean, organized, and on-brand.
  • What we're looking for: 2+ years of retail, food service, or grocery management experience; strong leadership and communication skills; flexible schedule including weekends and holidays; food handler's certification (or willing to get one); 18 or older.
  • Compensation: Competitive salary, commensurate with experience — discussed during interview. ~40 hours per week.

Part-Time Reports to: Store Manager

Weekly Cleaning Specialist

One scheduled day a week to help keep our store spotless. Flexible hours, friendly team, great for students or retirees.

  • What you'll do: Deep-clean the sales floor, meat case, back room, restrooms, and cellar. Follow food-safety and sanitation standards, and help the team stay ready for customers every day.
  • What we're looking for: Reliable, detail-oriented, and comfortable working around a food retail environment. Flexible scheduling — one day per week, with occasional flexibility for special events.
  • Compensation: Hourly, discussed during interview. One scheduled shift per week.
